Aloo Gobi is a zesty and comforting Indian side or main dish made with fresh cauliflower and tender potatoes.Aloo Gobi Sabzi – A Punjabi style dry version that’s sautéed with only a handful of simple ingredients. A rich and creamy restaurant style version, and a slightly lighter dry sabji variation.

Aloo Gobi is a rustic and satisfying dish that can be enjoyed as either a side or main entree. “Aloo” is the Hindi word for potatoes, and “gobi” means cauliflower. These two simple veggies are cooked with bold spices for a vegetarian – even vegan – recipe that is wholesome and simple but exciting to enjoy.



STEPS TO MAKE


1.Rinse and Peel - Rinse 1 medium sized cauliflower in water and chop the florets.Bil these in water with some salt. Peel 5 to 6 medium potatoes and slice into wedges.

2. In a kadai or thick bottomed pan, heat oil or ghee (clarified butter).

3. Lower the flame and add the potato wedges.

4.Stir the potatoes well and saute them on a low flame or sim for 6 to 7 minutes. Stir a couple of times when sauteing them for even cooking.

5. Add the cauliflower and sauté for about 3 to 4 minutes. Continue to stir in between.

6.Add4 teaspoons of chopped ginger. Stir again very well.

7. ½ teaspoon turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder and salt as required.

8 Combine and mix very well so that the spices are mixed uniformly in the sabzi and coat the veggies evenly.

10.Cover the pan with its lid tightly.

11.Simmer for 18 to 20 minutes on a low flame. Timing will vary with the size, depth and quality of the pan and flame intensity.

12. In between for a couple of times, remove the lid and stir well.

13.Once the potatoes and cauliflower have cooked, softened and have become fork tender, sprinkle ½ teaspoon garam masala powder and mix it with the entire sabzi.

Serve this dry aloo gobi sabzi with roti or paratha or bread and a bowl of curd (yogurt). The recipe also makes for a tiffin box brunch or lunch with a side of roti or bread. If you want you can garnish with a few coriander leaves. You can also serve this dish with any Indian main course.

Aloo Gobhi Recipe

Aloo gobi is a comforting, delicious North Indian dish made with potatoes and cauliflower.

Prep Time: 30 mins Cook Time: 30 mins Total Time: 1 hr Servings: 2-3

Ingredients:

  • 3 tablespoons oil or ghee (clarified butter)
  • 300 grams cauliflower or 1 medium sized cauliflower, florets removed and rinsed
  • 225 grams potatoes or 5 to 6 medium size potatoes, sliced in wedges
  • 1.13 inches ginger or 4 teaspoons chopped ginger
  • 0.38 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 0.75 teaspoon kashmiri red chili powder (or deghi mirch)
  • salt as required
  • 0.38 teaspoon Garam Masala
  • 0.75 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves for garnish – optional

Instructions:

  1. First soak the cashews in warm water for 30 minutes. Then drain and blend them to a smooth paste with about 2 tbsp water in a grinder or blender.
  2. Rinse and peel the potatoes and then dice them. Chop the onions and tomatoes finely.
  3. Rinse and chop the gobi/cauliflower into medium sized florets.
  4. Heat enough water with salt for the florets to be immersed in, on stove top or in the microwave.
  5. dd the rinsed florets and keep them immersed in the hot water for 15 to 20 mins. This is to to get rid of the insects or worms that might be in the cauliflower.
  6. Then drain the cauliflower and rinse once again with fresh water.
  7. Heat the butter and oil in a pan and add all the whole spices or whole garam masala listed above. Saute till the spices become fragrant.
  8. Then add the chopped onions and saute till they become golden.
  9. Add the ginger-garlic paste. Stir and saute till the raw aroma of the garlic-ginger goes away.
  10. Add the chopped tomatoes and sauté till the tomatoes become soft and pulpy. This takes about 5 to 7 minutes on a low to medium flame.
  11. Add the cashew paste and all the spice powders one by one – turmeric powder, red chilli powder and coriander powder.
  12. Stir and saute till you see fat coming from the sides of the masala paste.
  13. he masala paste will start getting together and you will clearly see the fat leaving the sides. This process takes about 8 to 10 minutes on a low to medium flame.
  14. Keep stirring often so that the masala does not stick to the pan.
  15. Add the cauliflower and the potatoes. Stir well. Then pour water and season with salt as required.
  16. Do add water as required. Where we live, I get cauliflower which takes more time to cook. Thus I add more water. If the cauliflower takes less time to cook, then add less water.
  17. Cover and cook the curry till the veggies are done but not falling apart. Stir for a few times when the veggies are simmering.
  18. If the gravy (sauce) thickens too much then just add a few tablespoons of water and simmer for a couple of minutes.
  19. Lastly add the garam masala powder, crushed kasuri methi and cream.
  20. tir and simmer the curry for half a minute without the lid. You can garnish with chopped coriander leaves or mint leaves if you prefer.
  21. Serve the aloo gobi hot dotted with cream or butter and sprinkled with a pinch of garam masala, accompanied with rotis, naan or steamed basmati or cumin rice.
