ANDA BHURJI RECIPE



By Naveen Sharma

Last Updated: 20 oct,2024

Egg Bhurji aka Anda Bhurji aka Khageena. A Pakistani/North Indian take on scrambled eggs. This recipe is precise yet adaptable, balanced in seasoning, and addictive in texture. Perfect for a simple but elevated breakfast or brunch. Tested to perfection!

Bhurji means scramble, so Egg or Anda Bhurji is just scrambled eggs flavored with aromatics and spices. I grew up with Desi-style omelets, which are more like frittatas in that you whisk the vegetables into the eggs. I was less aware of this spicier, less uniform version.



STEPS TO MAKE


1.Crack the eggs and whisk to combine. Adding cilantro and salt here helps evenly distribute them.

2.Heat oil in a nonstick pan. Sauté the onions to soften and bring out their natural sweetness. A bit of crisp around the edges is lovely, but we’re not browning them like we would for a curry.

3. Add garlic and green chili pepper to take the bite out and soften. I also add the remaining salt here to add flavor to the plentiful onions.

4.Reduce the heat and cook out the rawness (or kachapan) from spices. Add the tomato and sauté for a minute to soften but still keep its freshness.

5.Now you’ll pour in the eggs. Stir the eggs constantly, just until they’re cooked and no longer custardy or slimy.

6.Turn off the heat when they’re cooked but still soft.

7.Garnish with more cilantro if you’d like. Devour immediately!

Anda Bhurji|Egg Bhurji Recipe

Egg Bhurji aka Khageena. A Pakistani/North Indian take on scrambled eggs. This recipe is precise yet adaptable, balanced in seasoning, and addictive in texture. Perfect for a simple but elevated breakfast or brunch. Tested to perfection!

Prep Time: 15 mins Cook Time: 15 mins Total Time: 30 mins Servings: 2-3

Ingredients:

  • 4 (~250 g) large eggs, preferably organic, pasture-raised – quality eggs are key!
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped cilantro, plus more for garnish
  • 3/4 tsp kosher salt, divided – use less for sea salt/table salt
  • 3 tbsp neutral oil, I use grapeseed
  • 1 large (~250 g) yellow onion, diced/li>
  • 3 garlic cloves, finely chopped or crushed using a mortar & pestle
  • 1/2 Serrano or Jalapeno pepper, chopped or sliced – deseed for less heat, use up to 1 for a stronger kick
  • 1/4-1/2 tsp red chili powder or cayenne
  • 1/2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/4 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 (~60 g) small vine or Roma tomato, finely chopped

Instructions:

  1. Crack the eggs in a medium bowl and whisk until uniform. Add the cilantro and ½ tsp of the salt and whisk again to combine.
  2. Heat oil in a medium nonstick pan over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the onion and sauté for 5 minutes, until soft and translucent.
  3. Add garlic, green chili, and remaining ¼ tsp salt. Continue to sauté for another 2 minutes, until the onions start to turn golden on some edges. No need to brown the onions
  4. Reduce the heat to low. Add the spices (red chili, coriander powder, cumin powder, black pepper, and turmeric) and stir for 1 minute to allow them to bloom.
  5. Increase the heat to medium-high. Add the tomato and sauté for a minute, until softened.
  6. Pour the whisked eggs into the onions. Stir constantly until set but still glossy, about 2 minutes . Turn off the heat and garnish with more cilantro, if desired. Serve immediately with any type of bread, paratha, or roti.
